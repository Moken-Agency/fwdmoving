Moving Tips
& Best Practices

Moving Basics

  • The best time to book your move will depend on a few factors, including the season and current demand for moving services. In general, it's a good idea to book your move as far in advance as possible, ideally at least a few days before your desired moving date.

    We tend to be busiest at the end of the month, and do see more demand during the summer months.

    However, it's always a good idea to call and check availability with us, especially if you have a specific date in mind. You can also consider calling at the beginning of the month to start making arrangements and ensure that you get a spot on the schedule.

  • Moving into a new home can be a challenging and stressful process, but there are steps you can take to make it go more smoothly. One important thing to remember is to keep us informed about any potential challenges or special considerations that may come up.

    For example, if you have a piano that needs to be moved, be sure to let us know in advance so we can bring the necessary equipment. It's also a good idea to let us know about any height restrictions in your loading zone as well, as this could affect the type of truck we bring and potentially increase the time and cost of your move.

    By providing comprehensive information up front, you can help ensure a smoother, more successful move.

  • To ensure the most efficient and successful move, it's generally best to try and limit the amount of time the movers are working to 6-7 hours. This will help ensure that they can work at their best and avoid any potential issues related to fatigue.

    In most cases, you can expect that 2-3 movers will be able to handle your move, but if you have a large number of boxes or your current or destination location has a lot of stairs, you may need additional movers.

    If you're not sure how many movers you'll need, we recommend asking us to come and assess the complexity of your move. We can then recommend the appropriate number of movers and help coordinate the details to ensure a smooth and successful move.

  • To help ensure a smooth and successful move, here are a few things to consider:

    • If you need to use the elevator in your building, be sure to notify the building manager of your moving date and make any necessary reservations.

    • Make sure you have a proper parking spot reserved for the moving truck. This may require contacting the city to reserve a spot in advance.

    • Don't forget to document any existing scratches or damages on walls or floors and provide this information to your inspector. Taking pictures can be helpful for reference.

    • On daylight-saving dates, be sure to communicate with your moving company to confirm the correct time.

    • To avoid any injuries or damage to your pictures and paintings, it's a good idea to remove them from the walls and remove any nails or hooks before the moving day. You can also ask your movers to handle this for you on the day of the move.

  • There are many factors that can affect the length of time it takes to complete your move, and the best way to get a good estimate is to outline your situation as in detail as possible to help us work with you to assess your specific move. Some things to consider include:

    • The number of boxes you have to move

    • How prepared you are for the move (e.g. whether you still need to do any packing)

    • Whether you are moving from an apartment or a house

    • The distance between your home and the elevator, and the distance between the elevator and the moving truck

    • Whether the truck has a designated loading zone

    • The type and number of furniture items you have, and whether they require special attention or disassembly/reassembly

    • Whether the elevator at your current or destination location will be locked for the move

    By being as prepared as possible and making sure everything is ready to go, you can help minimize the time and cost of your move.

 

Item Specific Best Practices

  • To avoid any potential messes during the move, we recommend cleaning and drying your barbecue thoroughly before moving day. Barbecues can contain liquid fat and dirt that could potentially stain your carpet. While we take steps to protect your carpet during the move, it's always best to be proactive to prevent any potential accidents. By cleaning and drying your barbecue, you can help us ensure that everything arrives at your new home in the best possible condition.

  • To ensure the safe transport of your fridge and keep your food fresh during the move, here are a few tips:

    • Before moving day, make sure to unplug your fridge and leave it in an upright position for at least two hours.

    • If it's summertime, consider preparing some ice gel packs and placing them in your freezer. These will help keep your food cool during the move.

    • Keep a plastic cooler on hand and fill it with food and ice gel packs just before pickup. The movers will load it last, so it will be one of the first items unloaded at your destination. This will allow you to transfer the items to your fridge or freezer as soon as possible after arrival.

  • To ensure the safe and successful transport of your liquid items during your move, we recommend following these steps:

    • Group your bottles of liquids together and wrap them in an extra layer of paper or a used plastic bag to contain any potential spills.

    • Use bubble wrap, wrapping paper, or used cardboard pieces to further protect the bottles.

    • Cover cardboard boxes containing liquids with a plastic layer to prevent any leaks from damaging your furniture.

    • Use pieces of cardboard or layers of paper or towels to cushion the bottles inside the boxes.

    • Clearly mark the boxes as containing liquids and include an arrow to indicate the correct orientation.

    • Avoid overloading the boxes with more than 15kg or 33 pounds.

    • Consider using our free plastic moving boxes to help eliminate the need for additional packing materials and ensure the safe transport of your belongings.

  • To ensure the safety of everyone involved and protect your belongings during the move, we recommend not including the following materials on the moving truck: corrosives, ammonia, gasoline, bleach, propane tanks, paint, kerosene, explosives, or fireworks. For more information on how to properly pack and move liquids, please see the information above.

  • To ensure the safety of your fragile items during the move, we recommend properly wrapping them and labeling the boxes as fragile. This helps our movers handle them with extra care and avoid stacking them with other boxes.

    If you will need our team to pack your boxes for you, please let us know when you book your move so we can ensure that our packers arrive with the necessary supplies and take extra care with your fragile items.

    • Use uniform boxes for packing. We advise using our free plastic moving boxes to make your move easier. They are excellent, especially when you are moving in the rain.

    • Keep the contents of each box under 44 lbs. or 20 kg. Besides damage, moving heavy boxes is challenging, tires the movers quickly, and decreases the mover’s efficiency.

    • Use small boxes for dense items like books and larger boxes for lighter things like clothes.

    • Tape and secure all your moving boxes using cardboard boxes. Taping boxes prevent losing any small items. It also helps the movers stack, carry, and pack the boxes faster and easier.

    • Moving boxes with coloured stickers make their move to the right place more comfortable.

    • Pack fragile items like dishes with wrapping, packing paper, bubble wrap, and cardboard.

    • Avoid using newspaper as packing material as it leaves marks on the dishes or items.

    • Place delicate items in the middle of boxes to avoid damage on the side of your moving boxes.

    • To balance the weight of your moving boxes, pack clothes and fragile items in the same box.

  • To ensure the best possible care for your plants during your move, we recommend withholding water for two days prior. This helps to prevent any accidental spills during transport and also makes it easier to move larger plants. If you have any concerns about the well-being of your plants during the move, please don't hesitate to ask your movers if they have the proper tools and equipment to protect them.
